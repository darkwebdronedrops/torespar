## 2. Project Description

### Genre & Positioning

*The Tower of Tintantulos* is a **narrative deckbuilding roguelike** targeting PC (Steam, itch.io) with potential console ports. It occupies the intersection of:
- **Slay the Spire** (deckbuilding, roguelike structure)
- **Hades** (narrative progression across runs)
- **Into the Breach** (tactical positioning on a grid)

**Target audience:** Core PC gamers aged 18–35 who value strategic depth, narrative integration, and replayability. The game addresses a gap in the market: deckbuilders with genuine narrative stakes, where story progress persists across failed runs.

### Narrative Premise

The player is an Aspirant climbing a sentient clocktower fortress. Each floor is a distinct realm — fungal caverns, brass gearworks, crystalline gardens — each with its own guardian, its own story, and its own answer to the question: *why does the tower exist?*

The tower learns. It adapts. It remembers every death. And the Aspirant must decide whether to master it or become part of it.

### Core Mechanics

**Combat:** Turn-based deckbuilding on a hex grid. Cards represent attacks, defenses, utilities, and environmental interactions. Positioning matters — line of sight, terrain effects, enemy patterns.

**Progression:** Between-floor shops, card upgrades, relics (persistent modifiers), and character unlocks. Death is permanent but narrative progress persists — cutscenes, lore entries, and character relationships carry forward.

**Adaptive Content:** AI-assisted narrative generation creates contextual dialogue, environmental descriptions, and reactive story beats based on player behavior (cards favored, enemies killed, choices made). This is NOT procedural narrative — it is hand-authored content selected and contextualized by AI systems.

### Art Direction

- **Hand-painted pixel art** with parallax backgrounds
- **8 distinct environment themes** (10 floors, 2 floors share a theme)
- **Animated character portraits** for bosses and key NPCs
- **Particle effects** for card plays, environmental hazards, and magical effects
- **UI design** inspired by tarot cards and clockwork mechanisms

### Audio Design

- **160+ original tracks** — floor themes, combat music, boss themes, title/credits
- **Dynamic music system** — seamless transitions between ambient exploration and combat
- **Sound effects** — card plays, enemy attacks, environmental interactions
- **Full audio pipeline complete**; no licensing dependencies

## 3. Market Analysis

### Target Market

**Primary:** PC (Steam, itch.io, Epic Game Store)  
**Secondary:** Nintendo Switch, PlayStation, Xbox (post-PC launch)  
**Tertiary:** Mobile (adapted touch controls, post-console)

**Market size:** The deckbuilding roguelike genre has proven commercial viability:
- *Slay the Spire*: 5M+ copies, $30M+ revenue
- *Monster Train*: 500K+ copies
- *Griftlands*: 250K+ copies
- *Hades*: 1M+ copies (different genre but validates narrative + roguelike)

**Differentiation:** Tintantulos is the only deckbuilder combining hex-based tactical positioning with persistent narrative progression across runs. Most deckbuilders are mechanically excellent but narratively thin. Tintantulos delivers both.

### Competitive Landscape

| Game | Strength | Gap Tintantulos Fills |
|------|----------|----------------------|
| Slay the Spire | Deep mechanics, replayability | No persistent narrative |
| Hades | Narrative + roguelike | Not deckbuilding |
| Into the Breach | Tactical grid positioning | Not roguelike, no deckbuilding |
| Monster Train | Multi-lane strategy | No hex grid, limited narrative |
| Griftlands | Narrative + deckbuilding | No tactical positioning |

**Tintantulos:** Deckbuilding + hex tactics + persistent narrative = unique market position.
